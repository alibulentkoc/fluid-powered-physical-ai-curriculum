# Lab 01 — Equipment List

*Observing Pressure and Force in a Simple Hydraulic System*

This lab is deliberately low-cost and low-pressure. It can be run with kitchen and hardware-store items. Two build tiers are offered: a minimal version and a more accurate version.

---

## Minimal build (under $25)

| Item | Qty | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|
| Plastic syringe, ~10 mm bore (small) | 1 | Measure actual inner bore; record it |
| Plastic syringe, ~30 mm bore (large) | 1 | Larger area = visible force multiplication |
| Silicone or vinyl tubing, 4–6 mm ID | ~30 cm | Must fit snugly over syringe nozzles |
| Water | — | Tap water is fine for the minimal build |
| Known masses (coins, weights) | set | Or use labeled food items of known mass |
| Digital kitchen scale (0.1 g resolution) | 1 | Reads output force as mass × g |
| Ruler or tape measure | 1 | For piston displacement |
| Small clamp / stand / tape | — | To hold syringes vertical |
| Safety glasses | 1 per person | Required |

**Connection tip:** If tubing does not seal on the syringe nozzle, wrap with PTFE (plumber's) tape or use a drop of silicone sealant. Air-tight and water-tight is essential.

---

## Improved build (more accurate, ~$60–80)

Everything above, plus:

| Item | Qty | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|
| Luer-lock syringes with matching connectors | 2 | Cleaner sealing than friction-fit |
| Food-grade mineral oil | small bottle | More representative of hydraulic fluid than water; less corrosive |
| Small digital force gauge (0–50 N) | 1 | Direct force reading instead of scale method |
| Digital calipers | 1 | Accurate bore measurement (critical for area ratio) |
| Lab stand with clamps | 1 | Stable vertical mounting |
| Third syringe of a different bore | 1 | For the area-ratio variation (Part 5) |

---

## Optional — pneumatic substitute

For instructors who prefer air over liquid for cleanup reasons, a low-pressure pneumatic demonstration can illustrate the same area-ratio principle. **Important caveat:** air is compressible, so the displacement/energy-conservation portion (Part 4) will not behave like an incompressible fluid. This is itself a teachable contrast — note it explicitly to students, and keep pressures under 3 bar with properly rated fittings.

---

## What to measure and record before starting

- Small syringe bore diameter (mm), measured with calipers
- Large syringe bore diameter (mm), measured with calipers
- Calculated areas and the theoretical area ratio $A_{out}/A_{in}$

Bring these numbers to the procedure (`procedure.md`).

---

## Safety reminders

- Maximum pressure: 3.5 bar (50 PSI). This setup is not rated for more.
- Safety glasses on at all times.
- Never point a charged syringe at a person.
- Clean spills immediately, especially oil.

---

## Cost and sourcing notes

All minimal-build items are available from a pharmacy, hardware store, or kitchen supply. Syringes (without needles) are sold for crafts, baking, and pet care. No needles are needed or wanted for this lab.
